Overview

Plaid is an open banking API platform founded in 2013 by Zach Perret and William Hockey. The company connects consumer bank accounts to fintech applications via API, powering services like Venmo, Robinhood, Coinbase, Betterment, and H&R Block. Plaid serves 8,000+ customers and roughly half of all Americans with bank accounts use apps connected via Plaid. In 2021, Visa's $5.3B acquisition fell through due to DOJ antitrust concerns. In April 2025, Plaid raised $575M at $6.1B valuation (down from $13.4B peak in 2021). Revenue grew 25% in 2024 to ~$380M with positive operating margins.
