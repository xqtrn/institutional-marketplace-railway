Overview

China Aviation Lithium Battery (CALB) Group is a partially state-owned developer and manufacturer of lithium-ion batteries, ranking as China's third-largest and the world's fourth-largest EV battery supplier by installed capacity. Founded in 2007 as part of China Airborne Missile Academy (AVIC) and currently controlled by Changzhou city government, CALB became the first battery manufacturer listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in October 2022, raising $1.3 billion. In 2024, the company achieved 36.49 GWh of power battery installations in China, up 11% year-over-year, and expects net income to double to approximately RMB 786-874 million. CALB supplies batteries to major automakers and is aggressively expanding capacity with a goal to reach 1 TWh annual production by 2030.
