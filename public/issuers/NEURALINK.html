Overview

Neuralink is a neurotechnology company founded by Elon Musk in 2016 to develop implantable brain-computer interfaces (BCIs). The company's flagship device, the N1 chip, is a coin-sized implant that can read neural signals to enable people with paralysis to control computers and phones with their thoughts. In January 2024, Neuralink implanted its first human patient, Noland Arbaugh, who demonstrated the ability to play chess and video games using only his mind. The company received FDA approval for human trials in 2023 and has since expanded trials. Neuralink raised $680M+ at a valuation of $8.5 billion, with backing from Founders Fund, Google Ventures, and other investors.
