Overview

Bird Global was a pioneering electric scooter sharing company founded in 2017 by former Lyft and Uber executive Travis VanderZanden. The company was a leader in the micromobility revolution, deploying shared e-scooters in cities worldwide. Bird went public via SPAC merger in late 2021 at a valuation exceeding $2 billion, but faced mounting losses and declining ridership. The company's market cap dropped from $2B to $70M within 12 months of its NYSE debut. After exiting unprofitable markets, laying off 23% of staff, and multiple CEO changes, Bird filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in December 2023. The company secured $25M in DIP financing from MidCap Financial and sought to sell its assets through a bankruptcy auction process.
