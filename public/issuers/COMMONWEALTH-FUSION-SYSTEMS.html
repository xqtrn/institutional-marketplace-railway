Overview

Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS) is the world's leading private fusion energy company, spun out of MIT in 2018. CFS is developing SPARC, a compact tokamak designed to demonstrate net energy gain (Q>1) by 2027, and ARC, the world's first grid-scale commercial fusion power plant planned for Virginia in the early 2030s. The company has raised nearly $3 billion - about one-third of all private fusion investment globally - from investors including Google, NVIDIA, and Breakthrough Energy Ventures. In 2025, CFS signed landmark power purchase agreements with Google (200 MW) and Eni ($1B), and received $15M from the DOE Milestone-Based Fusion Development Program. CFS's high-temperature superconducting magnet technology has proven commercially viable, putting them on track to deliver fusion energy this decade.
