Overview

Coda is an all-in-one collaborative workspace that combines the functionality of documents, spreadsheets, and applications into a single platform. Founded in 2014 by Shishir Mehrotra and Alex DeNeui from Google and YouTube, Coda achieved a $1.4 billion valuation and was used by companies including Uber, Spotify, The New York Times, and DoorDash. In December 2024, Coda was acquired by Grammarly to combine Coda's collaborative document capabilities with Grammarly's AI-powered writing assistance. At the time of acquisition, Coda had $41 million in revenue and over 10,000 paying customers.
