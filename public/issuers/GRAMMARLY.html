Overview

Grammarly is an AI-powered writing assistant founded in 2009 by Ukrainian entrepreneurs Max Lytvyn, Alex Shevchenko, and Dmytro Lider. The company reached $13B valuation in 2021 and $700M+ ARR in 2025. In May 2025, Grammarly raised $1B in non-dilutive financing from General Catalyst. In December 2024, Grammarly acquired Coda and brought on Shishir Mehrotra (ex-YouTube VP) as CEO. The company is rebranding to Superhuman in late 2025 as it pivots from writing assistant to full AI productivity platform. Over 30 million daily active users across 500,000+ teams globally.
