Overview

Colossal Biosciences is the world's first de-extinction company, founded in 2021 by entrepreneur Ben Lamm and Harvard geneticist George Church. The company is using CRISPR gene-editing technology to bring back extinct species including the woolly mammoth (target: 2028), Tasmanian tiger (thylacine), and dodo bird. In January 2025, Colossal raised $200M at a $10.2B valuation, becoming Texas' first decacorn. The company has over 170 scientists across facilities in Dallas, Boston, and Melbourne, and has spun out two companies: Form Bio ($30M for computational biology) and Breaking ($10.5M for plastic degradation). Colossal's technologies have applications beyond de-extinction including artificial wombs, species preservation, and human healthcare.
