Overview

Clubhouse is an audio-based social networking app that pioneered the drop-in voice conversation format, enabling users to join live audio rooms on topics ranging from technology and business to music and politics. Founded in 2020 by Paul Davison and Rohan Seth, Clubhouse became a pandemic-era phenomenon, growing from zero to 100 million users in less than a year and reaching a $4 billion valuation. Backed by Andreessen Horowitz, DST Global, and Tiger Global, the platform raised $310 million in funding. While facing increased competition from Twitter Spaces, LinkedIn Audio, and Spotify's live audio features, Clubhouse continues to serve communities seeking authentic voice-based connections.
