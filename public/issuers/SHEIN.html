Overview

Shein is a Singapore-headquartered fast-fashion company founded in 2008 by Chris Xu (Sky Xu) in China. The company has become the world's largest fast-fashion retailer with 18% global market share, generating $38 billion in revenue in 2024 (up 23% YoY). Shein's AI-driven supply chain enables it to go from design to production in 3-7 days, adding thousands of new styles daily. The company has struggled with IPO plansâ€”after failing to gain SEC approval for a US listing, Shein filed with the London Stock Exchange in June 2024, gaining FCA approval in April 2025. In mid-2025, Shein also filed for a Hong Kong IPO. Net profit fell 40% in 2024 to ~$1 billion due to increased competition from Temu and rising tariffs affecting the de minimis exemption.
