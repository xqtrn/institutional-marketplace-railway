Overview

Bitmain Technologies is the world's largest designer and manufacturer of application-specific integrated circuit (ASIC) chips for cryptocurrency mining, controlling approximately 80% of the global Bitcoin mining equipment market. Founded in 2013 by Micree Zhan and Jihan Wu in Beijing, the company produces the Antminer series of mining hardware. Bitmain filed for a $1 billion Hong Kong IPO in 2018 at a $15 billion valuation, but the application lapsed in March 2019 amid Bitcoin price concerns and internal power struggles between co-founders. The company has expanded into AI chips through subsidiary Sophgo, though that entity was placed on the US Entity List in January 2025. Bitmain has made 45 investments in blockchain companies and continues to lead ASIC innovation with next-generation liquid-cooled miners developed with Hut 8.
