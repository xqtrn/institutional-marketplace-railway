Overview

Binance.US is the American cryptocurrency exchange affiliated with global crypto giant Binance, built specifically for U.S. regulatory compliance. Founded in 2019, the platform offers trading across 160+ cryptocurrencies with low fees and staking for over 20 assets. After facing an SEC lawsuit and loss of banking access in June 2023, the exchange operated crypto-only until February 2025 when USD services were restored under interim CEO Norman Reed, a former SEC enforcement attorney. In May 2025, the SEC formally dismissed its lawsuit with prejudice, marking a major victory for the platform. Binance.US maintains a 73-person team in Palo Alto and continues rebuilding banking relationships while expanding its product suite.
