Overview

Carbon is a Silicon Valley-based 3D printing unicorn that pioneered Digital Light Synthesis (DLS) technology, revolutionizing additive manufacturing beyond prototyping into production-grade applications. Founded in 2013 by Dr. Joseph DeSimone and team, the company combines molecular science with hardware and software to create industrial 3D printers capable of producing end-use parts at scale. Carbon's breakthrough partnership with Adidas produced the first mass-manufactured 3D printed midsoles in the Futurecraft 4D and subsequent 4DFWD lines. The platform serves industries including footwear, automotive, dental, and consumer products with a subscription-based business model.
