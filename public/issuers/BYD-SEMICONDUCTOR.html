Overview

BYD Semiconductor is China's largest automotive insulated gate bipolar transistor (IGBT) maker, supplying power semiconductors critical for electric vehicle powertrains. A subsidiary of BYD Company, the chip unit was spun off in 2004 and has emerged as a global competitor to Infineon and Mitsubishi Electric. The company develops IGBT modules, SiC MOSFETs, smart sensors, and optoelectronic semiconductors used in EVs, industrial applications, and renewable energy. BYD Semiconductor planned a ChiNext IPO in 2024 at a CNY 30 billion pre-money valuation, targeting up to CNY 150 billion upon listing. While the initial spinoff was scrapped in 2022 to focus on capacity expansion at the Ji'nan foundry, IPO plans have been revived with CICC as sponsor.
