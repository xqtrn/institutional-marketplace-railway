Overview

Cambridge Mobile Telematics (CMT) is the world's largest telematics service provider, measuring and protecting over 55 million drivers globally through AI-powered mobile technology. Founded in 2010 by two MIT professors, the company pioneered behavior-based insurance (BBI) and reached unicorn status with a $500 million SoftBank-led investment. CMT's DriveWell Fusion platform gathers sensor data from smartphones, connected vehicles, dashcams, and IoT devices to detect distracted driving, crashes, and risky behaviors. The company's 2024 Road Risk Report revealed an 8.6% decline in distracted driving - the largest drop in five years - helping prevent an estimated 55,000 crashes and $2.2 billion in economic damages annually. CMT earned Frost & Sullivan's 2024 Market Leadership Award for innovations in telematics insurance and connected claims.
