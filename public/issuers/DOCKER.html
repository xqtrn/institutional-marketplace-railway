Overview

Docker is the pioneering containerization platform that revolutionized how developers build, share, and run applications. Founded in 2013, Docker created the industry-standard container format that spawned the entire cloud-native ecosystem. With over 20 million developers and 14 billion image downloads, Docker Hub is the world's largest container registry. The company achieved a $2.1 billion valuation after focusing on developer tools following the 2019 sale of its enterprise business to Mirantis. Docker Desktop provides a seamless development experience on Windows and Mac, while Docker Hub enables teams to share and collaborate on containerized applications. In 2024, Don Johnson (former Oracle Cloud Infrastructure founder) became CEO, succeeding Scott Johnston, positioning the company for continued growth and potential acquisition interest.
