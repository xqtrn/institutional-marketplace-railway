Overview

Cruise is General Motors' autonomous vehicle subsidiary, acquired in 2016 for $1 billion with ambitions to build a profitable robotaxi fleet. At its peak, Cruise achieved a $30 billion valuation with investments from Microsoft, Honda, and institutional investors. However, after accumulating over $10 billion in operating losses and a critical 2023 incident where a pedestrian was dragged by a Cruise vehicle in San Francisco, GM announced in December 2024 it would exit the robotaxi business entirely. The company lost its California operating permits, laid off 900 employees, and cancelled its custom Origin robotaxi vehicle. GM is now integrating Cruise's technology into its personal vehicle ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) development, expecting to save $1 billion annually.
