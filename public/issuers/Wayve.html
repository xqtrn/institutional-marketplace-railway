Overview

Wayve is a British autonomous driving technology company founded in 2017 by Alex Kendall and Amar Shah, two machine learning PhD students from the University of Cambridge. The company pioneers Embodied AI for self-driving vehicles, using end-to-end deep learning that enables vehicles to learn from experience rather than relying on detailed maps or hand-coded rules. Unlike traditional autonomous vehicle systems that depend on expensive LiDAR sensors and high-definition maps, Wayve's approach uses primarily camera data and AI that can adapt to new environments without geofenced limits. The company was the first to deploy end-to-end deep learning autonomous vehicles on public roads. Headquartered in London with offices in California, Vancouver, Germany, and Japan, Wayve has raised over $1.3 billion from investors including SoftBank, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Uber. The company is currently in talks to raise an additional $2 billion at an $8 billion valuation. Wayve's vision is to create a "general purpose" driving AI that can power all levels of automation in any vehicle, anywhere in the world.
