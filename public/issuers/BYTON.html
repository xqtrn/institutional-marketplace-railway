Overview

BYTON was a Chinese-German electric vehicle startup founded in 2017 by former BMW and Nissan executives with ambitious plans to revolutionize smart mobility. The company garnered significant attention at CES 2018 with its M-Byte concept featuring a massive 48-inch curved dashboard display. Backed by China FAW Group, CATL, Foxconn, and other major investors, BYTON raised over $1 billion and reached a valuation near $2 billion. However, repeated production delays, internal turmoil, executive departures, and financial difficulties led to suspended operations in 2020 and eventual bankruptcy in 2023. Despite partnerships with Foxconn and substantial government backing from Nanjing, the M-Byte never reached mass production, making BYTON one of the most prominent casualties of China's EV startup shakeout that saw over 400 EV companies cease operations between 2018 and 2025.
