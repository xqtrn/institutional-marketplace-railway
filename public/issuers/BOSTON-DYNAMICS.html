Overview

Boston Dynamics is a world-renowned robotics company that designs and builds advanced mobile robots with remarkable mobility, agility, and autonomy. Founded in 1992 as a spin-off from MIT by Marc Raibert, the company has created some of the most sophisticated robots ever built, including the humanoid Atlas, the quadruped Spot, and the warehouse robot Stretch. In 2021, Hyundai Motor Group acquired 80% of Boston Dynamics for $880 million. In 2024, the company unveiled an all-electric version of Atlas, significantly stronger with a broader range of motion. In April 2025, Hyundai announced plans to deploy 'tens of thousands' of Boston Dynamics robots across its manufacturing facilities as part of a $21 billion US investment. Boston Dynamics also partnered with Toyota Research Institute to develop general-purpose humanoid robots using large behavior models.
