Overview

Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) is an American aerospace manufacturer and space transportation company founded in 2002 by Elon Musk. The company designs, manufactures, and launches advanced rockets and spacecraft with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling Mars colonization. SpaceX operates the Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy rockets, the Dragon spacecraft, and is developing the Starshipâ€”the largest and most powerful rocket ever built. The company's Starlink satellite internet constellation has become its largest revenue driver, with 2025 revenue projected at $13.3 billion. SpaceX is in talks to raise new funding at a $400 billion valuation as of July 2025, making it one of the most valuable private companies in the world. Total funding raised to date: $11.9 billion over 31 rounds from 229 investors.
